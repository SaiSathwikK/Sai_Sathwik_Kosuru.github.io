Synopsis

Stereotypical intuitions for ML Research lie in the evolution of newer, simpler, and more innovative architectural designs that succeed in improving learning rates and enhancing the predictability potential of respective models. While the current study revolves around similar concepts, it delves into the architectural and learning pattern differentiators across various models. In this work, we consider a one-fits-all multiclass data set of 120,000 coarse image representations on which we performed multiple rounds of hit-and-trial experiments to assess actual differences among their functioning, performances, and applications.

The way the experiments were conducted is relatively novel owing to the involvement of aspects like discrete partitioning of behaviors and functionality dispersion studies. We observed a significant relevance of our study in ML Research, which has 90% more impact than other studies conducted on a similar scale. Our results produced 88%-92% accuracy in differentiating and categorizing the considered models.
