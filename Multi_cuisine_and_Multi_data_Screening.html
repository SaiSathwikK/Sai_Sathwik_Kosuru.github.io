Synopsis

The food industry has seen unequivocal negligence for years due to vivid commercialization and fierce market competitions, leading to widespread ignominies since food is dependent on agriculture and health collectively. The major emphasis has been on food processing industries, but household-level applications still need to be developed. This work's main development is the optional use of the Internet of Things (IoT), as opposed to existing systems that implement multiple IoT devices to trace various physical aspects vis-Ã -vis food quality where economic feasibility is lacking.

We propose a computer vision-based model that uses the electromagnetic spectrum to trace the quality of all elements, including those obscured in the dish, and is integrable with various IoT and cloud applications. The model employs YOLO NAS, fine-tuned on four different datasets, leading to the development of state-of-the-art service point-based applications.

This model sets a new paradigm in IoT-driven AI computing by effectively circumventing the processing bottlenecks of its predecessors. By implementing specialized regular expression pipelines, this work enhances data extraction precision by 65% and promises an 80% accuracy increase in a star-topology network. This advancement is pivotal in improving the efficiency of AI systems across various applications, ensuring better handling of both numeric and non-numeric data metrics. The success of this model emphasizes the potential for future research and development in streamlining AI data processing methodologies.
