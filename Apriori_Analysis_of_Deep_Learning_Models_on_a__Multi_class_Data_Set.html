Synopsis

Deep Learning techniques have large scopes in terms of applications and resource requirements, hence calling for a deep analysis of their behavior. Many studies focus on the application part of these models, while relatively fewer discuss the unit-wise behavior. This research involves a multi-class data set and three different models trained, with their training processes described in terms of runtime characteristics like learning rate and times.

An apriori analysis investigates the runtime characteristics for their similarity with pre-defined knowledge of their architectures, contrasting with posterior analysis, which focuses on observations. The work also serves as a framework for defining the logistics of model training processes and fine-tuning the parameters devised in the future for maximum model accuracy.

Results are further converted into graphical representations for enhanced delivery of insights. This study is novel because, unlike previous works where performances are only tested, the model is evaluated in a design-specific manner, dealing solely with architectures and the parameters defining them.
