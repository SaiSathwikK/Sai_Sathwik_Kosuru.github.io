Synopsis

Though various solutions have been proposed regarding real-time data processing, user-defined fine-tuning, and repository-based hosting, Artificial Intelligence still grapples with unsynchronized data processing, thread-safe data storage, and isolation of numeric metrics from non-numeric ones. This poses a new question: How to amplify stream processing for AI to work in any application?

In this work, we implemented specialized regular expression pipelines that enable effective attribute retrievals with domain-specific metadata attachments for application use. Its novelty lies in how space- and time-expensive regular expressions are used to extract required data from large data streams delivered at a rate of 8000 data/minute via IoT systems. We demonstrated an appreciable increase in data extraction precision by 65%, and the solution promised an accuracy increase of 80% in a star-topology kind of network.

This model sets a new paradigm in IoT-driven AI computing, effectively circumventing the processing bottlenecks of its predecessors. By implementing these specialized pipelines, we have shown that it is possible to achieve higher precision and accuracy in data processing tasks. This advancement can be pivotal in improving the efficiency of AI systems across various applications, ensuring better handling of both numeric and non-numeric data metrics. The success of this model emphasizes the potential for future research and development in streamlining AI data processing methodologies.
