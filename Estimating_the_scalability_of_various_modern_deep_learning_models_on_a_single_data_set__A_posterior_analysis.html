Synopsis

Diversification of Learning Algorithms is critical to the functioning of smart computing systems where there is a need to derive insights and abstract complex architectures. By modeling the human brain, these networks and algorithms work on inputs through suitable weight and bias assignments. They undergo multiple iterations of learning, ultimately leading to the acquisition of input patterns. This research focuses on the scalability of such heavy applications across diverse data sets. Given the complexity of these models, they may easily overfit on smaller and simpler data sets. Therefore, a multi-class small-sized data source was considered to perform apriori and posteriori analyses of the networks. The research provides a clear picture of these algorithms' scalability and performance. In the initial stages, some algorithms failed, some overfitted, and others could not run when hardware resources were limited. Various ideas were considered in selecting the most suitable network. Ultimately, the research proposed critical aspects for considering system-cum-environmental scalabilities across Compilation & Platform Suitabilities, Architectural Dependencies, and Network Analyses.
