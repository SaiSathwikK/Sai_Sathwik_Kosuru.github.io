Implementing Feature Selection and Feature Engineering on internal organs is a tough task that requires complex statistical and numerical analysis of feature vectors across chromatic contours and density considerations of the input images. In the current work, we amplified the train dataset chromatic intensities by 65%, improving feature selection efficiency by 25%, and Computer Vision implementation effectiveness by 85%. We employed Computer Vision Modules like YOLO NAS, OrganAMNIST, OrganCMNIST, and TissueMNIST to produce an application that implements predictions on internal organs and tissues of the gastrointestinal tract up to the stomach for examining the proximity of ulcers and other diseases/comorbidities like diseases.

Our work produced an overall accuracy of 91.87% compounded as an ensemble accuracy. It was successful in detecting 99.87% of gastrointestinal diseases, producing a probable increase of 37% in the field of health computational sciences. The use of endoscopy for diagnosing and predicting gastrointestinal conditions has revolutionized the approach to handling internal organ health issues. The application of advanced computer vision techniques has not only enhanced the accuracy of diagnoses but also streamlined the process of monitoring and prognosticating potential health risks.

By leveraging the capabilities of YOLO NAS, OrganAMNIST, OrganCMNIST, and TissueMNIST, we have significantly improved the predictive power of our models. The increase in chromatic intensity and the subsequent boost in feature selection efficiency have played crucial roles in achieving these results. The findings from this study underscore the importance of integrating sophisticated data processing techniques with medical imaging technologies to advance the field of gastrointestinal health diagnostics.
