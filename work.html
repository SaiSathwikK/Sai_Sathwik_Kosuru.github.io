Description

During my 1-year Internship at IIITH, I delved into Hardware Data Acquisition, On-Spot Realtime Processing, and ML Modelling on bioimpedance data derived from Arduino sensors in a belt-like device. Specifically, I worked on a device which hosts 6 sensors that help identify the possibility for Cardiopulmonary Strokes through bioimpedance values.

Interfacing IoT data streams is an already researched task but hosting them on the android app is relatively novel. Additionally, this is an unbiased clinical knowledge that is investigated during my temporal skepticism studies wherein I removed biasing in the collected stream through selective clustering techniques.

Selective clustering is a standard clustering technique but consists of domain-specific metadata which involves subtle techniques to remove bias. I observed a remarkable performance improvement with this technique which is reflected through the below images.

The work produced 80% of the total project requirements and acted as a hub in the star topology network of IoT devices established thereafter. Furthermore, the pace of the project has been improved by 45%. The results were submitted for consideration at CVPR 2025.
